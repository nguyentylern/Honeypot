Your Online Privacy

What Privacy Means on the Internet

Image of a camera from Bernard Hermant on Unsplash.

Online, you are under constant oversight from the websites that you visit. These websites collect information about you like your time zone, operating system, browser settings and extensions, and other pieces of information in the form of cookies. These pieces are information are often spread around to track you. As a result, it is very difficult to escape your past on the internet.

Health

Is there a correlation between digital interaction and mental health?

Human beings are naturally social beings. They rely on real-world connections in order to ease on feelings they may be experiencing such as stress or anxiety. It can also boost one’s self-esteem and prevent feelings of loneliness. In the world we live in today most teenagers use social media as their main way to stay connected. Staying connected via Snapchat or Instagram has its merits but it is important to remember that social media can never replace real-world connections. The only way to relieve any negative feelings one may be experiencing is to have face-to-face contact. The research and studies done by many doctors and professors have proven that by not overusing social media to take over face-to-face interactions you can improve your overall happiness and mental well-being.

Since humans are such social beings they also depend on the action and emotion of others. If someone can reduce their internet usage and protect their internet privacy then they will be able to reduce signs of depression. Therefore they will uplift their community. In order to imporve public mental health you first need to improve your own mental health. Three changes to your daily activities that can improve your overall mood are, getting more sleep at night, you can reduce your social media usage, and you can connect and spent time with others. By following all of these tips you can improve your mental health and therefore the mental health of others.
