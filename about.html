What is Honeypot?

In computer terminology, a honeypot is a computer security tactic designed to mislead attackers away from actual targets (Johansen, 2022). As for this website, Honeypot is a project by three students at Innovation Academy (IA) in Alpharetta, Georgia. IA is a Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) school, and this project began as a prompt from an assignment in our GT Block class. The assignment tasked us with identifying and attempting to solve a problem based around certain prompts relating to the IT, healthcare, and engineering pathways. Our promt related to the excessive data collection by apps; we expanded upon this to include websites and other online services in general. This website is our attempted solution, and it addresses the problem by targetting the user directly, informing them so that they implement better practices to prevent their own privacy. In the GT Block class at IA, students participate in three aforementioned pathways, creating projects and completing assignments. This allows students to choose which pathway suits them best so that they can decide which to follow through their high school career.
